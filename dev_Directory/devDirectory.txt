
#----------dev Directory----------#

 1) /dev means The Linux kernel knows about these devices or device-interfaces and exposes them to the system.
 
 2) They do NOT always mean a physical device is plugged in right now.

 3) Wrong :  If it’s in /dev, the device is plugged in.
 
 4) Correct: If it’s in /dev, the kernel knows how to talk to it.
